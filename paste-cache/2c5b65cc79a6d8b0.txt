Short answer: there isn’t a single static “master list” anyone can print, because tickers, listings and definitions change. But you can get very close in practice by combining:

- a few **core ETFs/indices** per theme, and  
- 1–2 **screeners/directories** that maintain the full constituent lists.

Below is a practical map by industry, with (1) what to buy directly (stocks/ETFs) and (2) where to pull essentially complete lists yourself.

***

## 3. Climate Adaptation / Resilience

- **MAS** – Masco (building products; part of water/adaptation theme) [suredividend](https://www.suredividend.com/water-stocks/)

- Combine with **water and utilities universes** above, then filter for businesses where regulation/extreme weather is driving capex.

1. **Life science REITs & infra** (owning labs/biomanufacturing space):  
   - **ARE** – Alexandria Real Estate  
   - **EQIX / DLR** have some life‑science data overlap (less direct).

3. **Bioprocess equipment suppliers** (not facility owners, but linked to the same capex cycle):  
   - **DHR** (Danaher – Cytiva)  
   - **TMO** (Thermo Fisher)  
   - **RGEN** (Repligen)  
   - **SART.DE** (Sartorius)

- “Biotechnology facility engineering/construction” firms (CRB itself is private, but its client base points you to listed equipment vendors). [crbgroup](https://www.crbgroup.com/biotechnology)
- S&P or MSCI **Healthcare Equipment & Supplies** and **Life Sciences Tools & Services** sub‑indices, whose constituents are fully published by the index providers.

## How to Turn This into Your Own “Complete” Universes

For each theme you care about, you can get to a **practically complete, up‑to‑date list** using:

1. **Index/ETF constituents**  
   - Download holdings for PHO/FIW/CGW, ZAP, ARKX/UFO, BIZD, etc. – that gives you a curated investable universe for that theme. [fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/stock-market/market-sectors/consumer-staples/beverage-stocks/water-etfs/)

2. **Industry directories / screeners**  
   - Water: Fintel SIC 494 list, Investing.com “Water & Related Utilities”, SureDividend 46‑stock water list, InvestorIdeas water directory. [investorideas](https://www.investorideas.com/Water-Stocks/Stock_List.asp)
   - Private credit: the 45‑BDC list from StockAnalysis. [stockanalysis](https://stockanalysis.com/list/business-development-companies/)
   - Space: public SpaceTech company directory/indices. [analytics.dkv](https://analytics.dkv.global/spacetech/Publicly-Traded-Companies-in-SpaceTech-Industry-2021-Report.pdf)
   - Construction/grid: MarketBeat’s “Top 50 Construction Stocks” + grid modernization stock lists. [nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/5-top-stocks-to-invest-in-the-energy-grid)

3. **Your filters**  
   - From those raw universes, you can run your usual value screens (market cap, leverage, ROIC, region) and then build **personal watchlists** in your broker or a spreadsheet. That becomes *your* custom “complete list a retail investor can invest in.”
