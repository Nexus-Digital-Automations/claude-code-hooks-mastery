Here are the primary sources documenting Boomer wealth concentration and distribution:

**Tier 1 — Official Government/Regulatory Data**

1. **Federal Reserve - Distribution of Household Wealth in the U.S. since 1989**
   https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/z1/dataviz/dfa/distribute/chart/
   
   This is the authoritative source for generational wealth distribution. The Fed's Distributional Financial Accounts track wealth by generation quarterly, showing Boomers' share of total household wealth, equity holdings, and real estate. This is where the $78+ trillion and 52% of national wealth figures originate.

2. **U.S. GAO Report (GAO-06-718) - Baby Boom Generation**
   https://www.gao.gov/assets/a250908.html
   
   Congressional study documenting wealth concentration among upper-income Boomers and their likely decumulation behavior. Contains analysis showing wealthiest Boomers "will likely need to sell little, if any, of their assets."

**Tier 2 — Institutional Research**

3. **Wellington Management - US Generational Wealth Distribution**
   https://www.wellington.com/en-us/institutional/insights/us-generational-wealth-distribution
   
   Asset manager analysis of Boomer wealth concentration, equity exposure levels, and the "unusual" persistence of stock allocations among older generations.

4. **Investment Company Institute - 401(k) Research**
   https://www.ici.org/faqs/faq/401k/faqs_401k
   https://www.ici.org/system/files/2024-04/per30-03.pdf
   
   Data on retirement account balances and asset allocation by age cohort, including the 57% equity allocation among participants in their 60s.

**Tier 3 — Analytical/News Coverage**

5. **Fortune/Yahoo Finance - Boomer Wealth Share Analysis**
   https://fortune.com/2025/12/08/baby-boomers-wealth-gap-gen-z-millenials-great-wealth-transfer/
   https://finance.yahoo.com/news/baby-boomers-now-gobbled-nearly-185807558.html
   
   These cite the Fed data showing Boomers have "gobbled up" nearly one-third of America's wealth share.

6. **Self.inc - Generational Wealth Gap**
   https://www.self.inc/info/generational-wealth-gap/
   
   Analysis of wealth distribution across generations with visualizations of the concentration data.

The **Federal Reserve link is the primary source** you should reference for any rigorous analysis—it's the underlying data that most other sources cite.
